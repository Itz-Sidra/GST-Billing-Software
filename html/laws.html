Understanding GST Law

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a comprehensive indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services across India. It replaced multiple indirect taxes and brought uniformity to the tax system.

Key Benefits
  • ✓ Single tax system
  • ✓ Eliminates cascading effects
  • ✓ Digital compliance
  • ✓ Input tax credits

GST Acts and Rules

Central GST Act 2017: The Central Goods and Services Tax (CGST) Act, 2017 governs the levy of GST in India. This comprehensive legislation provides the framework for tax administration, registration, returns, and compliance procedures.
Integrated GST Act 2017: The Integrated Goods and Services Tax (IGST) Act regulates inter-state transactions, imports, and exports. It ensures seamless flow of input tax credits across state boundaries.
State GST Act 2017: Each state has its own SGST Act to manage intra-state transactions. These acts are largely uniform across states to maintain consistency in GST implementation.
GST Compensation Act 2017: Ensures states receive compensation for revenue loss due to GST implementation. This act provides a safety net for states during the transition period.
